Welcome!

I am a PhD student in the Political Science Department at Columbia University. My primary research interests are in the history of political thought, "computational political theory" (Natural Language Processing + Network Analysis + Political Theory), and the politics of the Middle East.

Before coming to Columbia, I worked as a Research Economist in Urban Economics within the Department of Economics and Haas School of Business at UC Berkeley.

Prior to UC Berkeley, I completed my MS in Computer Science at Stanford University. My first year at Stanford, I worked as a Research Assistant in the Stanford Network Analysis Project and in my second year I worked as a Research Assistant in the Department of Economics.

As an undergraduate student at University of Maryland, I studied Mathematics, Computer Science, and Economics.

In my free time I enjoy creating hip hop music, reading, and practicing Spanish, French, and Arabic. Feel free to email me with any questions or comments!
